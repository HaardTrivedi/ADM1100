The Future of Electric Cars

01

Background

  • Current & Future Goals
    The auto industry was established in the late 1800s with the number of customers increasing annually. At this point, cars have almost become a necessity for humans in everyday life. However, most cars are still fueled by gasoline, which is produced from non-renewable resources, and harmful to the environment when cars use them (as a source of energy) and emit greenhouse gasses (mostly carbon). In recent years, a major focus of all companies has been creating cars that have a lesser negative ecological impact and therefore one of the popular and “greener” solutions to this issue was the introduction of hybrid and electric vehicles which are now considered to be the future of the automobile industry. However, there are currently few problems with why all cars have not completely moved to electric vehicle industry yet.
02

Problem

  • Problem Overview
    The main difference between electric and gas-powered cars lies in the fact the electric vehicles are battery operated. The rechargeable battery is a major portion of the vehicle as all power is supplied through this device.

    Battery Issues
    Currently, car batteries are formed primarily of lithium and thus, called lithium-ion batteries. Unfortunately, lithium-ion batteries deteriorate over time. Additionally, essential minerals in the making of rechargeable batteries such as praseodymium, lanthanum, dysprosium and neodymium are rare and injurious to health.

    Another material, which can be harmful that is used in production is cobalt. Cobalt is a major part of the battery but there are ethical issues surrounding the mining practices utilized to obtain it. 50% of world's cobalt used is mined in the Republic of Congo and there are accusations of on-going child labour practices used for mining. Cobal can be harmful to humans and lead to severe heart conditions and vision problems.

    Infrastructure Drawbacks
    The term infrastructure alludes to the machinery and locations required for the electric vehicles to be successful. Due to its limited mileage - around 500 km - a lot of car charging locations are required and in frequent occurrence. There are not enough stations present in the world. Currently, there are only about 22,000 fast charging stations across the USA and Canada.

    Another hurdle is that the battery charging is very slow. It takes 1hr 25mins to charge from 0%-100% making road trips nearly impossible. The current batteries do not pack the necessary energy by weight as they should, making them inefficient. Consumers for electric cars expected to rise from three million to 125 million by 2030. The number of locations for power restoration of the vehicles will have to increase as well as the time taken to charge a car fully will have to be reduced.

    Undesirable Outcomes
    Currently, the number of users of electric cars is increasing at a steady rate of 22.3% annually. That number may dwindle to 16% with the decrease in the demand, which would have a negative impact on the economy of the country as the market would not be profitable. The job market would also be affected since the unemployment figures would rise. And the humanitarian crises would continue without a permanent halt or solution.
