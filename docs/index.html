The Future of Electric Cars

01

Background

  • Current & Future Goals
    The auto industry was established in the late 1800s with the number of customers increasing annually. At this point, cars have almost become a necessity for humans in everyday life. However, most cars are still fueled by gasoline, which is produced from non-renewable resources, and harmful to the environment when cars use them (as a source of energy) and emit greenhouse gasses (mostly carbon). In recent years, a major focus of all companies has been creating cars that have a lesser negative ecological impact.
02

Problem

  • Battery Issues
    Outdated lithium-ion batteries are still being used that deteriorate over time.

    Additionally, minerals in the batteries such as praseodymium, lanthanum, dysprosium and neodymium are rare and dangerous to human health but currently have no alternatives.

    Cobalt is a major part of the battery but 50% of world's cobalt used mined in Congo with human rights issues srrounding the mining practices.


    Infrastructure Drawbacks
    There are not enough car charging stations present in the world. In Canada and USA, only 22000 stations present. More are needed as the cars give mileage of approx. 500km.

    Battery charging is very slow. It takes 1hr 25mins to charge from 0%-100%.
